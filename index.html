The Maker Residency Program

Join us in this exciting journey to preserve, innovate, and celebrate the old while embracing the new, creating a future where technology and tradition coexist in harmony, and inspiring a new generation of creators and audiences alike.

What is Maker Residency Program ?

Technology has always had a profound impact on society, transforming the way we live, work, and create. The world of art is no exception. The tools we use have continually transformed the way we express and communicate emotions, ideas, and stories. Art has evolved through our acquired knowledge of tools - Language, canvas, photographs, television, and the Internet. Technology has consistently augmented our capacity to convey art and creativity, and this evolution continues today. technology has the magical power to extend the reach of art to a wider audience and enhance its ability to communicate effectively. Whether through the creation of immersive digital experiences, interactive installations, or modern interpretations of classical forms, the fusion of these disciplines can lead to groundbreaking work that honors the past while pushing the boundaries of the future.

The Maker Residency Program is a unique initiative that invites artists, makers, technologists, and innovators from around the globe to explore this intersection of art and innovation. Our program will give you access to collaborate with local artisans, engage with the most cutting-edge technology, and immerse yourself in a vibrant cultural landscape. Together, we will explore how digital tools and emerging technologies can be harnessed to enhance the narrative, aesthetic, and emotional impact of traditional arts.

Program Details

Duration: 6 months with an option to extend for another 6 months based on the project.

Starting Date: October 1st, 2024

Participants: Artists and Makers

Focus Areas: Integrating art and design with new-age technology in fields such as fashion, fine arts, performing arts, interactive installations, sustainability, etc.

Support and Benefits

Stipend: Rs 25,000 per month for the fellows selected for the residency program.

Facilities: Studio space, access to fabrication tools, consumables, and mentorship.

Collaboration: Opportunities to work with local artisans and technologists.

Exhibition: The work will be exhibited at Huddle Global 2024 and KLF 2025.

Important Dates

Application Deadline: 28th September 2024.

Residency Start Date: 28th September 2024.

Residency End Date: 28th September 2025.

The residency program aims to create innovative art-tech projects that can be showcased in exhibitions or public installations, inspiring a new generation of creators and audiences. These projects will serve as examples of how traditional art can be preserved and reinterpreted through the use of new media. By blending art and technology, participants will push the boundaries of creative expression, making a lasting impact on both the art world and its audience.

Additionally, the program will document and share project outcomes, encouraging further exploration in the art-tech space and fostering collaboration among artists, designers, and technologists. This initiative will contribute to building tech-driven design and art communities within the state, enhancing skills, exposure, and professional development for participants, and ultimately nurturing a new generation of skilled professionals.

F A Q ?

What are the expected outcomes of the residency?

Participants will create innovative art-tech projects for exhibitions or public installations. These projects will inspire new creators, preserve traditional art through new media, and foster collaboration among artists, designers, and technologists. The goal is to build tech-driven art communities that enhance skills and develop future professionals.

What is the selection process for the residency?

Participants are chosen through a portfolio evaluation and interview process. The selection is based on creativity, relevance to the program’s focus, feasibility, and impact. The panel includes representatives from technology firms, startups, maker communities, and KSUM leadership.

How can I apply for the residency?

Interested candidates can apply by submitting an online application form. The application requires personal information, portfolio links, a detailed project proposal, a timeline, and references. Applications are evaluated on creativity, feasibility, and alignment with the program's goals.

What facilities and resources will be provided?

Residents have access to fully equipped studios, digital fabrication labs, and traditional craft workshops. Additionally, we offer technical support, mentorship, and collaboration opportunities with artisans and technologists.

What is the community and collaboration experience like?

Our residency encourages collaboration with local communities. Participants can engage in cultural exchanges, public performances, exhibitions, and workshops, fostering a rich environment for growth and learning.
